We are all set for the lesson? Then let's begin. Thermodynamics is the study of heat flow between objects during a process, and quantities that are helpful to describe such a situation. Statistical Mechanics is analyzing a large number of physical objects or particles not by considering each one individually, but rather by considering statistical averages taken across the group.
Virion
And it may seem at first glance that these two subjects are entirely unrelated, but a potentially surprising fact is that when you try to analyze physical systems in a statistical mechanics way, the results you obtain correspond exactly to the results of thermodynamics. The statistical averages that we care about turn out to be exactly the thermodynamic quantities relating to heat.
